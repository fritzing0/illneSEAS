Diabetes

Type 1 does not make insulin while type 2 does not make or use insulin well. Gestational diabetes usually occurs in some women who are pregnant but can go away after birth. There is more of a chance to get type 2 later in life. Additionally, 9.4% of people in US have a type of diabetes.

Symptoms can include increased hunger, fatigue, blurred vision, numbness or tingling in the feet or hands, sores that do not heal, or unexplained weight loss.

Support and resources can be found on the following website. https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/communication-programs/ndep/health-professionals/game-plan-preventing-type-2-diabetes/help-patients-make-lifestyle-changes-after-prediabetes-diagnosis/support-your-patients-with-resources-referrals
